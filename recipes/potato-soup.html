Snow Days Potato Soup Recipe

Backstory

In a small, picturesque village, nestled among emerald hills and golden fields, there lived a kind-hearted woman known to all as Grandma Lily. She was a beacon of warmth and wisdom, and her cozy kitchen was a hub of laughter and storytelling. The most cherished of her culinary creations was her potato soup, a recipe steeped in history and passed down through generations. Each spoonful was a blend of tradition and comfort, lovingly crafted to warm both body and soul.

This soup was more than just a meal; it was a symbol of Grandma Lily's nurturing spirit. On chilly evenings, the villagers would gather around her table, finding solace in the rich, heartwarming flavors that seemed to chase away the coldest of winters. The recipe was a well-guarded secret, known only to her and whispered to the next in line when the time was right.

It was said that the soup held magical qualities, imbued with the love and care of all who had contributed to its legacy. The soup wasn't just a dish served at meals; it was an integral part of the village's identity, a thread that wove through the tapestry of their shared history, bringing together families and friends in a celebration of togetherness and community.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bacon
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 8 potatoes, diced
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 gallon whole milk

Directions

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, cook the bacon until evenly brown, but not crisp. Mix in the onion, and coat with the bacon drippings. Stir in potatoes, and season with salt and pepper. Mix in the flour and water, stirring until smooth. Cover pot, and cook 10 minutes, until potatoes are tender.
  2. Reduce heat to low. Gradually stir in the milk. Simmer soup 1 hour, stirring frequently to avoid scorching.
  3. Serve!
